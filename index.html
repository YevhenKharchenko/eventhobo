Big cities have shows.
Small places have soul.

Eventhobo helps you discover and sync with independent cultural gatherings across Europe — in vineyards, woods, rooftops, ateliers, rural stages. Not via ads.
Through rhythm, locality, and presence.

You don’t buy tickets.
You join routes.

Sync by feeling. Move by meaning.

  • Discover events not by date, but by vibe and region

  • Build a micro-festival route across 3–7 locations

  • Host or suggest events as curators or artists

  • Use “flow match” to align with people traveling your way

  • Download printable maps with notes, paths, time gaps

This is slow culture for fast souls.

Not all gatherings look the same.

Eventhobo tracks and maps:

  • Fireside jam sessions

  • Book-and-walk salons

  • Off-grid sound rituals

  • Forest movement workshops

  • Village cinema nights

  • Visual art open barns

  • Hidden rooftop DJ sets

Each has a symbol, rhythm profile, and suggested dress/mood.

The map isn’t static. It pulses.

An interactive scrollable map showing:

  • Active event routes

  • Walking/biking segments

  • Flow density (where people are headed)

  • Open calls for collaborators

  • “Pause nodes” — where nothing happens, and that’s the point

Map updates weekly

Your residency is the road.

Apply as a roving artist to:

  • Contribute to events along the route

  • Offer your art or skills in exchange for stay

  • Record, share, and document fragments of culture

  • Receive small travel grants from local networks

  • Be featured on our “ongoing artist mosaic”

The event ends. But the story echoes.

A rotating feed of:

  • “I played a silent violin set under fig trees in Istria. It lasted one hour. The dog howled once.”

  • “We met in a bookstore attic, and now we run a café together.”

  • “Someone taught me three Spanish words I’ll never forget — in a monastery hallway.”

You don’t need a plan. Just a signal.

Start by choosing:

  • Where you are now

  • What kind of rhythm you’re drawn to

  • Whether you’re attending, hosting, or seeking collaboration
